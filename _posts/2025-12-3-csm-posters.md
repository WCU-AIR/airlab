On Wednesday, December 3, The AIR Lab's student members presented their research posters at the College of Sciences and Mathematics's Fall Student Poster Presentation Event. The students' research posters include:
Video Over RTP Enhancements and Testing Methods (Sebastian Tran and Huy Nguyen), RAGTox: Toxicity Prediction using GraphRAG (Raga Mouni Batchu), Prompt-Driven AI Feedback for Introductory CS Courses (Chrisma Ndlovu and James May), The Impact of Prompt Engineering on Code Generation Accuracy and Hallucination Patterns in Language Models (Kadin Matotek), Emerging Trends in Large Language Model Use for Undergraduate Introductory Programming Education (Brian Kominik), and  Virtual Sensor Fusion: Leveraging Real-Time Data for Motion Analysis (Thomas Burke). 

Congratulations to Brian Kominick for receiving the 1st Place Award among graduate students for his poster presentation!
